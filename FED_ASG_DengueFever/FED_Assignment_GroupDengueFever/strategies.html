Curbing The Spread Of Dengue


After reading through what effects Dengue can have on your body, I am sure you now know the seriousness of this issue. Furthermore, what makes it more frightening is that no cure for Dengue has been created yet. Dengue is also a highly infectious disease. It has been reported that the total number of reported cases for Dengue reported was around 35000 in 2020.

Thus, dengue is a realy serious issue in Singapore and everyone needs to play their role in stopping dengue before it is too late. Even the Government has made efforts to stop the spread of Dengue, but it is not enough alone. Hence, every citizen in Singapore must play their part in stopping the spread of Dengue.

In this website, I will share some of the efforts made by the goverment organisations, as well as some methods each indivudal can do to fight against Dengue.

Efforts Made By The Government

Hiring NEA dengue inspection officers


Previous Next

NEA dengue inspection officers can be distinguished from their NEA Authority Card. They conduct activities such as outdoor fogging to kill adult mosquitoes. Once the equipment is prepared, the fogging starts from 9am to 9.30am as that is the period where the Aedes Mosquitoes are the most active.

The officers also conduct door-to-door inspections. When asking permission to enter one's home, the NEA Authority card will be presented and the purpose of their visit will be stated. The officers would do a quick check around the house to identiy areas that could be potential breeding habitats for mosquitoes. After identfying such areas, the officers would then start to inspect these areas thoroughly to search for any indication of mosquito breeding.

If a mosquito breeding habitat has been found, the officers will use an empty vial they carry to collect a sample of the larvae. This sample will be sent to the NEA laboratory for testing to find its type of species. NEA officers will then request for the person's identification and a fine will be issued after a certain period.

Furthermore, NEA officers uses treatment such as Ultra-Low Volume(ULV) Misting, a concentrated insecticide, in these houses to kill adult mosquitoes.

Releasing of Male Wolbachia-Aedes mosquitoes


Wolbachia-Aedes mosquitoes are not natural species of Aedes mosquitoes. The only way Aedes mosquitoes can carry the Wolbachia is when the bacterium is initially injected into the Aedes Mosquitoes eggs. The eggs will eventually become an adult Aedes mosquito that carries the bacteria. When a Male Aedes mosquito carrying the Wolbachia bacterium mates with a female Aedes mosquito that is not carrying the Wolbachia bacterium, the eggs produced by the female Aedes mosquito will not hatch, which will start to reduce the Aedes population over time, as well as reduces the spread of Dengue.

Since Wolbachia can be maternally transmitted, the bacterium can be passed down to the mosquitoes' offspring. Thus, this allows for large production of male Wolbachia-Aedes mosquitoes. Furthermore, male mosquitoes neither bites nor transmit diseases and thus a population of only Male Aedes mosquitoes will be ideal to stop the spread of dengue. Thus, when breeding Wolbachia-Aedes mosquitoes, females, whose pupae are larger than males, are usually removed.

As the name suggests, the Five-Step Mozzie Wipeout consists of 5 steps in which users can follow to get rid of stagnant water to remove the breeding habitats of mosquitoes. The Five-Step Mozzie Wipeout is based on a short"10-minute" routine made by NEA to help with the fight against Dengue.

The first step of the routine is to turn over all water storage containers such as pails and watering cans. This ensures that there are no stagnant water stored in such containers. The second step of the routine is to change the water in vases on alternate days. This is due to the fact that mosquito eggs can stick to the roots of the flowers easily and is not easily noticed unless it is cleaned.

The third step of the routine is draining the water from flowerpot plates once every two days. This prevents stagnant water and dirt from accumulating. The fourth step is to cover bamboo pole holders daily to prevent accumulation of rainwater, which if left undetected, can create a habitat for mosquitoes. Lastly, the fifth step is to clear blockages and place insecticide in roof gutters monthly.

Mosquito Repellent


One of the most important way to prevent the spread of dengue is through the use of Insect Repellent. Protecting oneself from the disease is the first step in reducing the spread of dengue. If a mosquito were to bite an infected person, the mosquito itself will be infectious and will carry the dengue strand. The mosquito will then bite others, causing others to be affected. Thus in order to reduce the spread of dengue, one must protect himself first. The best way to do so is to regularly spray insect repellent.

It is recommended to spray insect repellent in dark area around the house such as as under the bed as mosquitoes are attracted to dull coloured objects. Mosquito repellent patches should also be applied frequently to prevent mosquitoes from entering the house. It has been said that repellents that uses substances such as DEET, picaridin or IR3535 as the active ingredient are the most effective in repelling mosquitoes, so be sure to get a couple for yourself and your family!
