About dengue

Dengue virus is transmitted to humans with the bite of an infective mosquito. It is a common illness in more than 100 countries around the world, with 40% of the world's population living in areas with a higher risk of dengue. Dengue is counted as one of the serious disease as every year, up to 400 million people get infected with dengue and approximately 100 million people get sick, and 22,000 died from severe dengue. As there are 4 types of dengue virus (DENV1 - 4), a person can be infected with dengue virus up to 4 times in his/her lifetime.

How dengue is transmitted

  • Humans get infected with dengue fever by getting a bite of an infected Aedes mosquito.
  • Symptoms may show after a period of 3 - 14 days.
  • Dengue fever DOES NOT spread from person to person.
  • Infected pregnant woman may transmit the virus to their fetus during pregenancy.

About Aedes Mosquito

  • There are 2 types:
    • Aedes Aegypti
    • Aedes Albopictus
  • Both have black and white stripes on their bodies and legs, and narrow black body
  • There is a slight difference in size
  • They are the main culprit of spreading viruses like denuge, zika, chikungunya, etc
  • Mosquitoes become infected when they bite a person infected with the virus.
  • As they prefer to feed on people, they are more likely to spread viruses compared to other mosquitos
