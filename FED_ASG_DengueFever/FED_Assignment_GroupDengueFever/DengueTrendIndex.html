Trend of dengue in Singapore

Throughout the world there are, about 100 million dengue cases occur each year across around half of the world’s population. Dengue had claimed 20,000 deaths yearly, more ubiquitously found in Southeast Asia and the Pacific. Along with the spread of Dengue, Chikungunya and Zika has been viral too, especially in hyperendemic dengue areas. [1]

Details for the spike of Dengue

The most prominent

Since Dengue are more ubiquitously found in Southeast Asia and the Pacific, there is a prominent increase in deaths and cases in general. For example, India as more than 30% increase in both cases and deaths. Southeast Asia is expected to have the fastest spread of the disease. [2]


The reason for the increase

Especially in 2020, there has been a massive spike to the dengue fever disease, alongside COVID-19. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, it could be the main reason for the massive increase in Dengue cases due to the Stay-Home Notices (SHN) and lockdowns of countries such as Singapore. With citizens being cooped up at home and the worry of contracting the pandemic, they tend to forget about other important diseases such as Dengue. They, thus, let their guard down and have not taken the necessary precautions to prevent them from contracting Dengue.

Moreover, the increase in cases and deaths could be possible due to the increase in climate's temperature and increase in the number of construction sites, said Professor Tikki Pang, a visiting professor at the Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy. He added that there are changes in mosquito habits or a possibility of a more rapid-spreading form of dengue. [3]


Singapore's cases

According to the National Environmental Agency (NEA), around January to March 2019, a spike could be seen. About 60% of the cases are seen from the increase in breeding grounds, more ubiquitously the homes of citizens. The agency added that with "improper housekeeping measures in citizen's respective premises" (NEA, 2019), there would be a potential increase in breeding. [4]

From January to April, the agency found over 6,400 breeding habitats after holding about 300,000 inspections islandwide. [5]





With warmer weather facilities, the faster multiplication of the Aedes mosquito and the dengue virus, increasing the chances of dengue transmission. Moreover, with the concurrent COVID-19 outbreak and the "circuit breaker" in Singapore, dengue had spiked in May 2020 along with the circuit breaker measures, said NEA. [6]









With the rainy season in December 2020 and January 2021, there will be a spike in dengue cases and during December 2020, there has been a 8% increase in cases and is remaining high at some areas of Singapore. As such, 52 dengue clusters have been identified in areas such as Jalan Boon Lay and Tampines Place. NEA also added that discarded materials could serve as mosquito breeding habitats as well. [7]
